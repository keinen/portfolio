Selling Design

Selling design starts with good business

Over the years I have worked in web, I have discovered one of the most important things about selling design is that we sell solutions to real business problems that have a real impact.

Recently I was in a meeting with a software partner company when one of my colleagues says “You just do pretty stuff, we do the real heavy lifting”

It gets my gears stuck when people refer to the things we do as the ‘pretty stuff’ and when they do, I know I have done a bad job of communicating what it is we actually do.

We solve complex business problems for organisations of all sizes. It’s not just pretty pictures and buttons.

Its varying stakeholder needs, ambiguity and constraint, context, peoples livelihood and businesses we take into our hands.

All organisations have problems that impact on their bottom line or organisational objectives. Weather they have a direct financial impact or an indirect financial impact.

Direct financial impact: - We aren’t selling enough of product x Indirect financial impact: - There is not enough awareness that bullying affects everyone

With the web being the place more and more people turn to to make the best decision they can, we have a responsibility to the people who hire us to look at their business from all angles.

via GIPHY

Our job is to use the tools and technologies we have available to us to help organisations solve those problems. It’s like a test or exam. Read the question and understand it before solving it.

First we listen. Listen to as many stakeholders that the problem touches possible. Where possible talk to the organisations customers and audience.

Then we understand. We take what we have heard and think about it. Understand the whys’ and the hows. We may even have more questions so we listen again.

Then we look for a solution. A measured solution that takes into account what we have heard and understood. A solution that solves a business problem using the design tools we have available to us.

Selling design is about solving problems businesses have. In solving problems, measured against tangible impacts we move away from selling pretty pictures and lift the profile of what we do.
